A little bit about me...

I was born in London, England and moved to the East Coast of the United States at the young age of 5. I grew up in a small town in the Northwest corner of Connecticut. For my Senior Project in High School, I did an apprenticeship at a glassblowing studio called Bristow Studio Glass. From the very first moment I gathered hot glass, I knew I wanted to pursue glassblowing. I have dedicated almost my entire adult life to learning the craft of blowing glass. It is a truly magical material and I am very fortunate to have found my passion at such a young age. I spent my early twenties traveling and experiencing different cultures and places, until I found the right one. And moved to Portland, Oregon in 2004 where I buckled down to develope and continue to improve my skills as a glassblower.

I am interested in learning how to code because...

I started getting interested in learning to code when I realized the city I love and call home (Portland, Oregon) was beginning to change drastically and very quickly. Being a crafter/artist, I knew that the city was going to spit me out if I did not learn a skill that pays a living wage. In an ever changing economy I realize I have to play the games the rest of the world play. I also think computers, the internet, and programming languages are the wave of the future, and until the apocalypse happens, they will be a driving force in all sectors of society. There is also a lot of similarities between glass and coding. They both take a lot of time and effort to learn. And once you feel you understand, you realize you do not, which I like, it just makes you want it more. So in an effort to remain in our fair city and continue to make glass art, I am pursing another skill to add to my bag of tricks.
